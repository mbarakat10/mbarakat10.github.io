Maryland volleyball bottom of the league in aces

Author

Marwa Barakat

Published

October 24, 2024

Maryland volleyball fell behind most NCAA teams last season with subpar aces statistics.

In an analysis of last season’s active players with above average aces per match, the few Maryland players reaching this level are outperformed by most players across various positions.

Only three Maryland players from last season finished with more than 25 aces – which about a quarter of players across the league reached.

The three players – Erin Engel, Sam Csire and Lilly Gunter – are a setter, an opposite hitter and a defensive specialist, respectively. When compared to players in the same positions, the Terps were on the lower end of average for aces throughout the season.
